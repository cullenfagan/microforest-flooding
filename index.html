Could microforests be the answer to increased flood risks on the Bronx waterfront?

Corporate sustainability groups are pushing a trendy forestation technique as the solution to eroding urban coastlines, but experts aren't so sure.

By Cullen Fagan

November 30, 2023

Bronx, New York — In September, floods swept the city, reminding New Yorkers of the importance of coastal resiliency. According to experts, the Bronx is most at risk for hazardous flooding.

If more and more floods are expected to hit the Bronx, rapid solutions will be necessary to mitigate damage.

Waste Management, the private waste transfer facility along the Harlem River, planted a microforest this October in an effort to help fortify the Bronx waterfront.

Microforests grow at 4x the rate of normal forests, and increased forestation can help with coast resilience as tree roots lock up the soil and act as a natural barrier to rising river water and prevent inundation.

This is WM's 3rd microforest, and its first in New York City. To facilitate planting, they contracted the Wildlife Habitat Council, who assist private companies with microforest implementation nationwide.

However, not all experts are as sold on micro forests as a magical solution.

“People are really jumping on the bandwagon, and there isn't a lot of rigorous scientific information on how to do it,” said Richard Hallett, a research ecologist for the U.S. Forestry Service.

Regardless, according to Hallett, “even one tree” can help environments with climate resilience.
